SELF-BILLINGAGREEMENT

This Self-Billing Agreement (the “Agreement”) is made between GETFIXFY LTD, a company incorporated in England and Wales under company number 15406523, registered office 124 City Road, London EC1V 2NX (the “Self-Biller” or “Fixfy”), and the undersigned Partner (the “Supplier”).

It sets out the terms on which Fixfy will issue self-billed invoices on behalf of the Supplier for Jobs completed through the Fixfy platform, in accordance with HMRC self-billing requirements.

What self-billing means. Under a self-billing arrangement, the customer (Fixfy) prepares the supplier’s invoice and sends a copy to the supplier with the payment. The Supplier agrees not to raise its own sales invoices for the transactions covered by this Agreement.

1.  Agreement to Self-Bill

1.1  The Supplier agrees that Fixfy may issue self-billed invoices for all Jobs completed by the Supplier through the platform for the duration of this Agreement.

1.2  The Supplier agrees not to raise sales invoices for the supplies covered by this Agreement.

1.3  This Agreement takes effect on the date of acceptance and remains in force for a period of twelve (12) months, automatically renewing for successive twelve-month periods unless terminated earlier in accordance with clause 5.

2.  Supplier Obligations

The Supplier agrees to:

  • accept self-billed invoices raised by Fixfy on the Supplier’s behalf until this Agreement ends;
  • notify Fixfy immediately if the Supplier changes its VAT registration number;
  • notify Fixfy immediately if the Supplier becomes, or ceases to be, registered for VAT;
  • notify Fixfy immediately if the Supplier transfers its business, or part of it, as a going concern;
  • not issue its own VAT or sales invoices for the supplies covered by this Agreement; and
  • keep copies of all self-billed invoices it receives.

3.  Fixfy Obligations

Fixfy agrees to:

  • issue self-billed invoices showing the Supplier’s name, address and, where applicable, VAT registration number, together with all particulars required for a valid VAT invoice;
  • include on each self-billed invoice the statement “The VAT shown is your output tax due to HMRC” where the Supplier is VAT registered;
  • issue a new self-billing agreement if the Supplier’s VAT registration number changes;
  • send a copy of each self-billed invoice to the Supplier and retain a copy; and
  • not reclaim input tax on invoices that are incorrect or where this Agreement has ended.

4.  VAT and the Agency Model

4.1  Fixfy acts as a disclosed agent introducing Jobs between Clients and the Supplier. The Supplier supplies its Services to the Client; Fixfy provides agency, coordination and platform services and charges commission.

4.2  Where the Supplier is VAT registered, the self-billed invoice will reflect the VAT due on the Supplier’s supply. Fixfy’s commission is accounted for separately, with VAT applied to the commission as required by law.

4.3  The Supplier is solely responsible for accounting to HMRC for any VAT and income tax due on its supplies.

5.  Duration and Termination

5.1  Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time by written notice (including through the platform).

5.2  This Agreement automatically ends if the Supplier’s circumstances change such that self-billing is no longer permitted under HMRC rules, including a change of VAT registration number until a new agreement is made.

5.3  On termination, the Supplier resumes responsibility for issuing its own invoices for subsequent supplies.

6.  Governing Law

6.1  This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales and operated in accordance with HMRC self-billing requirements (VAT Notice 700/62 as amended from time to time).
